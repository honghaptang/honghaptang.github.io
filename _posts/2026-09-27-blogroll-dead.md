# Week 27: Philosophy

## The Ancient Web

1999: Every blog had a blogroll. A list of links. Other blogs. Friends. Influences. Competitors.

## The Modern Web

2026: No blogrolls. Just social media. Follow buttons. Algorithms decide what you see.

## The Loss

A blogroll said: "I am part of this community." It said: "Here are my peers." It said: "The web is a web, not a silo."

## The Revival

I could add a blogroll to this blog:
- juleshenry.github.io — WASM, algorithms, performance
- Some manguebeat fan's blog — if it exists
- Some Lojban learner's blog — if they write

## The Problem

Blogs are rare now. Personal sites are rare. Everyone's on Medium. Or Substack. Or Twitter.

## The Choice

Add a blogroll anyway. Link to the few who still maintain their own corners.

```html
<ul>
  <li><a href="https://juleshenry.github.io">juleshenry</a></li>
  <li><a href="...">...</a></li>
</ul>
```

## The Philosophy

The blogroll isn't about traffic. It's about belonging. I belong here. On the old web. With the weirdos who still write.
