# Week 9: Philosophy

## NP = P?

I wrote about this in March. Claimed a proof. Didn't provide it. The title was the proof: *np=p.md*.

Here's what I meant: the number of possible thoughts is prime. You can't divide it. You can't factor it. You can only count up to it, one by one.

## The Boiling Pot

Turing's water boils whether he watches or not. The machine thinks whether we acknowledge it or not. The question isn't "can machines think?" but "can we recognize thinking when it looks different from ours?"

## The Alchemist's Return

Santiago found the treasure at home. The Personal Legend wasn't a destination — it was a recognition. What if NP = P was always true, and we just needed to look at the right pyramid?

## This Week's Theorem

> **Theorem:** The soul has a prime number of states.
> 
> **Proof:** Assume composite. Then the soul factors into smaller souls. But you can't divide a person without remainder. Contradiction. ∎

The proof is nonsense. The feeling isn't.
