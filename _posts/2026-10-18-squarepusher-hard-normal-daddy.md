# Week 30: Music

## The Album

*Hard Normal Daddy* (1997) by Squarepusher. Tom Jenkinson. Bassist. Programmer. Madman.

## The Sound

Drum and bass meets jazz fusion. At 180 BPM. With live bass.

```
Track 01: "Beep Street" — 175 BPM, melodic
Track 02: "Hard Normal Daddy" — 180 BPM, chaotic
Track 03: "Tommib" — 140 BPM, beautiful (later sampled by Footcrab)
```

## The Technique

Jenkinson plays bass live over programmed breakbeats. Not quantized. Not grid-locked. Human.

## The Mathematics

At 180 BPM:
- One bar = 60/180 × 4 = 1.33 seconds
- 16th notes = 1.33 / 4 = 0.33 seconds apart
- Human reaction time = ~0.2 seconds

Playing 16th notes at 180 BPM is at the edge of human capability. Jenkinson lives there.

## The Connection

Aphex Twin's 50 Cycles. Goldie's Timeless. Squarepusher's Hard Normal Daddy.

The pattern: push the tempo. Push the technique. Push the form.

## The Legacy

*Hard Normal Daddy* proved electronic music could be played live. Could be virtuosic. Could be jazz-adjacent.
