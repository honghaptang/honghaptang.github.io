# Week 39: Music

## The Album

*Tri Repetae* (1995) by Autechre. Sean Booth and Rob Brown. Manchester. IDM pioneers.

## The Sound

```
Tempo: 130 BPM (slow for electronic)
Elements: Sine waves, clicks, glitches, minimal percussion
Mood: Clinical, precise, beautiful
```

## The Philosophy

Autechre doesn't write songs. They write systems. The music emerges from rules.

```
Rule 1: Use only sine waves
Rule 2: Quantize everything to 1/16
Rule 3: Vary one parameter per bar
Result: Tri Repetae
```

## The Tracks

1. **"Eutow"** — The opener. Four notes. Hypnotic.
2. **"C/Pach"** — The hit. As close as Autechre gets.
3. **"Gnit"** — The closer. 13 minutes. A journey.

## The Connection

Burial's *Untrue* is maximal emotion. Autechre's *Tri Repetae* is minimal emotion. Both are masterpieces.

The difference: Burial wears his heart on his sleeve. Autechre hides theirs in the code.

## The Legacy

*Tri Repetae* proved electronic music could be intellectual. Could be cold. Could be warm underneath.
