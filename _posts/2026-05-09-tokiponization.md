# Week 7: Multilingual

## The Smallest Language

Toki Pona has ~120 words. Maybe 137 if you count the obscure ones. No word for "computer" — you say *ilo sona* (tool of knowledge). No word for "blog" — maybe *lipu toki* (talking paper).

## The Translation Game

Take this thought: *"I'm frustrated by the API's country code validation."*

In Toki Pona:
> *mi pilike ike. ilo li ken ala sona e ma mi.*

I feel bad. The tool cannot know my country.

The frustration isn't in the words — it's in what's missing. Toki Pona can't express technical specificity. But maybe that's the point.

## What Each Language Demands

| Language | Forces You To Notice |
|----------|---------------------|
| Korean | Social hierarchy, relationships |
| Lojban | Logical structure, predicates |
| Portuguese | Vowel quality, rhythm |
| Toki Pona | Essence, not detail |

Toki Pona is the ultimate reduction. If you can't say it with 120 words, maybe you don't understand it yet.

## The Experiment

This week I tried thinking in Toki Pona for 10 minutes. The result:

> *mi lukin. mi sona ala. mi wile sona.*

I look. I don't know. I want to know.

That's the entire scientific method. Three phrases.
