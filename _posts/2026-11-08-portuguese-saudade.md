# Week 33: Multilingual

## The Word

*Saudade* (noun): A deep emotional state of nostalgic or profound melancholic longing for something or someone that one loves. Often with the reknowledge that the object of longing might never return.

## The Etymology

From Latin *solitas* (solitude) or *solitudo* (loneliness). Or maybe from Arabic. The etymology itself has saudade — a longing for an origin that may not exist.

## The Usage

```
Tenho saudades de você.
-- I have saudade of you.
-- I miss you (but deeper, sadder).

Saudades do Brasil.
-- Saudade of Brazil.
-- Longing for home.
```

## The Comparison

| Language | Word | Approximate Meaning |
|----------|------|---------------------|
| Portuguese | saudade | Nostalgic longing |
| German | Sehnsucht | Yearning |
| Welsh | hiraeth | Homesickness |
| Korean | 한 (han) | Sorrowful longing |
| English | — | No equivalent |

## The English Gap

English has no word for saudade. English speakers say "miss" or "long for." Something is lost in translation.

## The Blog's Saudade

This blog has saudade for:
- Posts I haven't written yet
- Posts I'll never write
- The readers who might come

Saudades do futuro.
