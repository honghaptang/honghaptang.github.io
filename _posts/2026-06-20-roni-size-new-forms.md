# Week 13: Music

## The Album

*New Forms* (1997) by Roni Size & Reprazent. Mercury Prize winner. The moment drum and bass went mainstream without selling out.

## The Tracks That Matter

1. **"Brown Paper Bag"** — The bassline that launched a thousand subwoofers
2. **"Share the Fall"** — Christian Jode's vocals over breakbeat science
3. **"Heroes"** — The title track that isn't about heroes

## The Mathematics

At 170 BPM:
- One bar = 60/170 × 4 ≈ 1.41 seconds
- A 4-minute track = ~170 bars
- Each bar contains: kick, snare, hi-hats, bass, samples

That's 170 opportunities to get it right. Or wrong.

## The Live Experience

Reprazent performed live: drums, bass, vocals, keys. Not a laptop in sight. This was 1997. DAWs existed but weren't ready. They played *live*.

## The Connection

Manguebeat did the same thing in Recife. Chico Science & Nação Zumbi played maracatu with electric guitars. Live. Real. Human.

The pattern: take electronic music, make it human again.
