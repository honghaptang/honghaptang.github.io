# Week 4: Books & Philosophy

## The Alchemist Gets Misread

Paulo Coelho's *The Alchemist* is often dismissed as airport self-help. That's too easy.

The book's actual thesis is stranger: **the universe conspires to help you achieve your Personal Legend**. Not your goal. Not your five-year plan. Your *Legend* — the thing you're meant to do before you die.

Santiago's journey teaches:
1. The treasure isn't where the map says (it's back where you started)
2. The journey transforms the traveler (the boy becomes the alchemist)
3. Love liberates, doesn't anchor (Fatima waits, but doesn't chain)

## Desire as Engine and Obstacle

We want AGI. We can't fetch news from Cameroon via API.

We want treasure. We dig at the wrong pyramid.

Desire propels and blinds. The Personal Legend isn't found by following desire — it's found by following the *right* desire. The one that survives repeated disappointment.
